# VERG — Turning Paper into Pathways
 
> A framework for digital inclusion built for communities whose lives exist on paper.
 
---
 
## What is VERG?
 
VERG is a four-pillar framework that addresses digital inclusion for people whose records — identity documents, land deeds, income history, community papers — exist as physical documents, handwritten records, or photographed images rather than clean digital files.
 
Most digital systems are built assuming people already have legible, formal, structured records. For the majority of the world, that assumption is false. VERG closes the gap between paper lives and digital futures.
 
---
 
## The Four Pillars
 
### V — Voice
*Whose records get recognised?*
 
Systems must be able to read the documents people actually have — not just the documents designers imagined they would have. This means supporting handwritten records, multilingual scripts, low-resolution photographs, and documents that are old, degraded, or informally issued.
 
### E — Equity
*Whose proof counts?*
 
Verification standards must accept imperfect and informal proof alongside clean digital originals. A crumpled passbook and a PDF bank statement prove the same thing. A hand-drawn land boundary and a registered coordinate describe the same place. Equity means the standard of proof does not quietly privilege those who already have institutional access.
 
### R — Reach
*Where does the system go?*
 
The digitisation pipeline must travel to where the paper is — not require people to travel to where the server is. This means offline-first tools, submission via channels people already use, scanning points at community locations, and trusted local networks that make the system reachable without a journey.
 
### G — Growth
*What does access unlock?*
 
Connection without opportunity is a window without a door. Growth means converting digitised records into real economic participation — credit, welfare, markets, scholarships, and formal employment — so that the internet becomes a ladder, not just a mirror of existing inequality.
 
---
 
## The Problem We Address
 
> *"Rapid technological change without an inclusive and sustainable development strategic orientation risks entrenching existing inequalities while introducing new ones."*
> — UN DESA Policy Brief #92
 
Digital inclusion programmes typically focus on connectivity and devices. VERG focuses on legibility — whether the evidence of a person's life is readable to the systems that make decisions about them.
 
A person is not digitally included when they have a smartphone. They are digitally included when their documents are recognised, their proof is accepted, their submission can reach the right place, and access translates into opportunity.
 
---
 
## Who This Is For
 
- Communities where critical records exist as physical or photographed documents
- Regions where documents are written in scripts underserved by current technology
- Populations whose economic history is informal and invisible to digital systems
- NGOs, governments, and developers building tools that need to work for the hardest cases first
---
 
## Framework Principles
 
1. **Design for the hardest case first.** A system built for the most excluded will work for everyone else automatically.
2. **The document is not the problem.** If a system cannot read a legitimate record, the system is the problem.
3. **Proof is not the same as format.** The validity of evidence must be separated from the tidiness of its presentation.
4. **Reach is infrastructure.** The distance between where a document exists and where it needs to be submitted is a cost paid almost entirely by the poorest.
5. **Access is not the destination.** Inclusion is only meaningful if it leads somewhere.
